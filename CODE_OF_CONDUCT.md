# Code of Conduct

This document defines the standards that the project should adhere to. Due credit is provided to the [Rust](https://github.com/actions-rust-lang/.github/blob/master/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) and [Zig](https://ziglang.org/code-of-conduct/) code of conducts.

It is a living document — that is, it will continue to be edited and updated to reflect changing social norms and in response to incidents.

Our goal here is to create a stress-free and constructive environment both within our own codebase and for the game itself.

## No Generative AI

[mental gymnastics]: https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/mental_gymnastics

Under zero circumstances should you use Large Language Models (a form of Generative AI) for any form of written text. To curb any form of [mental gymnastics], the following is a non-comprehensive list of examples:

- Do not create additional burden for developers by submitting AI generated code.
- No documentation provided by Large Language Models. The ability to write documentation implies an understanding of the code.
- No ideas or suggestions generated by Large Language Models. It is not feedback provided by you, it is low effort spam that you have forwarded to us.
- Refrain from finding bugs solely using Large Language Models, that is what testing and reproduction steps are for.
- Refrain from using Large Language Models for translation, prefer human translation whenever possible.

For other forms of Generative AI, we would like to quote the relevant paragraph from `ARCHITECTURE.md`

> - [Generative AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/30/what-a-joke-github-copilots-new-token-based-billing-spurs-consternation-among-devs/). [Not Cheap](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/02/uber-caps-employee-ai-spending-after-blowing-through-budget-in-four-months/). Subsidized.
> - [We do not condone environmentally destructive technologies](https://www.unep.org/news-and-stories/story/ai-has-environmental-problem-heres-what-world-can-do-about). Until Generative AI providers can resolve this real issue it is against the message of the game.
> - As their sources can not be tracked, we must assume that their training data includes uncredited sources / stolen works. This is plagiarism, this will create more issues than it *pretends* to solve.
> - They do not provide an editable source file. This inherent limitation makes it not usable for any serious use as an asset within a production pipeline especially with their uncanny appearance.
> - [AI generated content is not eligible for copyright](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/03/19/ai-art-cannot-be-copyrighted-appeals-court-rules.html).
> - As platforms such as Steam require you to declare whether or not you've used Generative AI, by using Generatie AI you declare to players you are willing to sacrifice quality by taking (expensive) shortcuts a la [Doorman Fallacy](https://www.jaakkoj.com/concepts/doorman-fallacy). In simple words, it makes the game feel cheap and lazily made wtihout any semblance of actual care put into it.
> - Simple writing is a basic skill and we consider prompting an AI image generator as low effort. Nobody wants to play [AI slop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI_slop) no matter how many times you'd like to gaslight yourself or be in denial otherwise.

## Mind Your Words / Behaviors

[**Read**: Paradox of Tolerance](https://www.freiheit.org/mexico/limits-tolerance-poppers-paradox)

In the mission of creating a stress-free and inviting space, there are guidelines to follow how one should not only speak but also in how they act. This is not being "holier than thou" or being "woke", these are just guidelines on how to be a decent person (or in other words, how to make sure your vibes are not repulsively rancid)

Examples of misconduct include:

- Intentionally harmful conspiracies or rhetoric intended to demonize marginalized groups or encouraging to cause harm. (e.g: claiming "they're injecting chemicals into kids")
- Publishing others' personal identifiable information without their explicit and definitive consent / permission. (i.e: don't dox others)
- Destructive behavior intended to harass, slander, or otherwise harm a person, a group, or another software project.
- Disruptive or troublesome behavior intended to cause discomfort. (e.g: screaming  slurs, targeted attacks, nonconsensual sexual attention / advances, etc.)
- Support of beliefs intended to oppress or otherwise silence the innocent and those in need.

Examples of good behavior include:

- Be respectful of others' characteristics — avoid making disparaging comments on one's age, body, disabilities, ethnicity, sex, gender, level of experience, socio-economic status, nationality, appearance, race, and religion.
  - Being inexperienced is not an excuse for lacking transparency and communication.
- Showing empathy — the ability to imagine being in one's shoes — towards others. This differs from sympathy — showing concern for others, but the latter is also a good characteristic to have.
- Assisting someone else accomplish a given task to the best of your abilities or guiding them towards the right path.
- Respecting others' boundaries and being able to give them personal space if needed.

Feel free to curse / swear, just not targeted towards others nor in a hateful manner.
